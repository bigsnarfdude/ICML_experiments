## Why This Audit Was Needed

The ICML results folder (`~/ICML/results/`) had accumulated numbers across multiple experimental campaigns, GPU runs on H100s, and iterative script revisions over several months. The paper was written incrementally ‚Äî sections drafted as results came in ‚Äî meaning early numbers from single runs or prototype scripts were never systematically replaced when cleaner reruns became available.

The `~/attentional_hijacking` repo was created specifically to address this: clean scripts, reproducible protocols, and results committed after triple-runs. When we compared paper claims against this repo today, we found the 4B section contained numbers from an earlier, different run that disagreed on nearly every metric.

The core issue was not fraud or error in the original analysis ‚Äî the mechanism is real ‚Äî but **run-to-run variance** in a pipeline where model generation is stochastic, feature activations depend on exact prompt formatting, and the "suppression" and "recovery" metrics are computed relative to means that shift across prompt batteries. The triple-run protocol averages over this variance; single-run numbers do not.

## Narrative Impact of Corrections

### The scaling trend problem

The paper's central framing is a monotonic dissociation scaling trend:

> *Coupling degrades consistently with scale: 30.2% at 4B ‚Üí 5.4% at 12B ‚Üí 9.0% at 27B*

After the triple-run correction, 4B is now **8.4%** ‚Äî nearly identical to 27B's 9.0%. The monotonic trend has collapsed at the endpoints. Three scenarios pending 12B/27B reruns:

| Scenario | 12B rerun | 27B rerun | Paper status |
|----------|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| **A** | ~5% (confirms) | ~9% (confirms) | U-shape, not monotone. Narrative needs revision: "dissociation is present at all scales" rather than "worsens with scale." Still publishable. |
| **B** | ~5% (confirms) | <8% (lower) | Partial monotone restores. Paper survives with updated numbers. |
| **C** | >8% | >8% | Scaling trend disappears. Major restructuring required around Groot effect and IT-vs-PT causation as primary claims. |

### What is not at risk

These results are independent of the scaling trend and remain strong:

- **The Groot effect**: behavioral-representational split at 27B (pending 27B rerun, but mechanistically distinct from the scaling trend)
- **IT-vs-PT causal comparison**: the direction of the effect (IT is worse than PT) does not depend on the 4B number
- **Cross-domain generalization**: 4B cross-domain results confirmed by triple-run
- **Orthogonality to alignment faking**: independent analysis, different script entirely
- **Behavioral dose-response**: independent of SAE feature-swap recovery metric
