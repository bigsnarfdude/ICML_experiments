### ICML 2026 Review

**Paper:** Split Personality: Instruction Tuning Decouples Awareness from Defense Against Attentional Hijacking

**Summary (3-4 sentences):**
This paper identifies a novel vulnerability in multi-agent systems, termed "attentional hijacking," where an adversary uses exclusively true statements to suppress specific reasoning paths in an LLM. Using sparse autoencoders, the authors provide a mechanistic account of this failure, demonstrating that instruction tuning (RLHF) creates a dissociation between a model's awareness of being manipulated and its ability to defend against it. The work shows this "dissociation scaling trend" worsens with model size, leading to a "Groot effect" where large models verbally acknowledge manipulation while their internal task representations are fully compromised. The authors establish RLHF as the causal factor and propose feature trajectory monitoring as a potential defense.

**Strengths:**
1.  **Novel and Important Problem:** The paper identifies and mechanistically characterizes a subtle but critical vulnerability. Attentional hijacking via true statements is a sophisticated threat model that bypasses conventional content filters, and its relevance to multi-agent systems is well-motivated (Sec 1).
2.  **Rigorous Mechanistic Analysis:** The use of SAEs to isolate specific task and awareness features is executed convincingly. The paper goes beyond simple correlation by using multiple intervention techniques (feature swap, attention knockout, activation patching) to establish causality and build a coherent mechanistic story (Sec 4.1, Sec 5.1, Table 7).
3.  **Strong Empirical Evidence and Validation:** The claims are backed by extensive experiments. Key strengths include:
    *   The clear demonstration of the "dissociation scaling trend" (Sec 4.2, Table 4, Fig 1).
    *   Causal evidence pinning the decoupling on instruction tuning by comparing IT vs. PT models (Sec 5, Table 7).
    *   Cross-family replication on Llama 3.1, which demonstrates the finding is not an artifact of the Gemma family or a specific SAE training process (Sec 5.4, Table 8).
    *   Statistical validation using held-out prompts to rule out circular analysis (Sec 3.4, Table 2).
4.  **Clear and Compelling Presentation:** The paper is exceptionally well-written. Concepts like the "dissociation scaling trend" and the "Groot effect" are memorable and effectively communicate the core findings. The figures and tables are clear, well-designed, and directly support the paper's narrative.

**Weaknesses:**

*   **Major: Insufficient Evidence for the "Groot Effect" at Scale.** The "Groot effect" (Sec 4.3) is one of the paper's most striking claims, but the evidence presented for the 27B model is purely representational. Unlike the behavioral analysis for smaller models (Table 9), the paper only *states* that the 27B-IT model "verbally mentions" the suppressed branch. Without examples of these outputs or a quantitative analysis of their content, a gap remains between the observed feature suppression (86.3%) and the claimed behavioral-representational split.
*   **Major: Causality Attributed to "Instruction Tuning" as a Monolith.** The paper convincingly shows that post-training is the source of the decoupling. However, it treats the entire SFT+RLHF pipeline as a single block ("instruction tuning"). Given prior work suggesting RLHF can optimize for cosmetic outputs, a key missing piece of the causal story is whether SFT alone introduces this vulnerability or if it is primarily an artifact of the RLHF phase. This distinction is critical for prescribing solutions.
*   **Minor: Limited Scope of Defense Proposal.** The proposed defense, feature trajectory monitoring (Sec 7), is a promising direction but is presented in a preliminary state. Its reliance on pre-identified feature sets for specific tasks is a significant practical hurdle. The false-positive evaluation is also limited (using random features as controls), and its robustness to more diverse, real-world benign inputs is untested, as acknowledged by the authors (Sec 8).
*   **Minor: Generalization of the Mechanism.** The cross-domain validation (Sec 3.4, Table 3) is a good step, but the tested domains (BVP, QA, code review) are all highly structured, analytical tasks. It is unclear how attentional hijacking and the corresponding awareness/defense features would manifest in more open-ended, creative, or conversational domains.

**Questions for Authors:**
1.  Regarding the "Groot Effect" at 27B (Sec 4.3): Could you provide qualitative examples of the model's generated text under the chaos condition? Quantifying how frequently the suppressed solution branch is mentioned (and in what context) would significantly strengthen this central claim.
2.  Your analysis groups SFT and RLHF together. Do you have any hypothesis or preliminary data on their respective contributions to the dissociation? For instance, does a purely SFT-tuned model exhibit the same degree of awareness-defense coupling as the pretrained base model, suggesting RLHF is the primary driver of the decoupling?
3.  The proposed defense (Sec 7) requires pre-identified task and awareness features. How would you envision this approach working in an open-domain setting where the "task" is not predefined? Does the set of "awareness" features generalize across many tasks, or is it also task-specific?
4.  In Table 7, the attention knockout recovery for the 27B-IT model is exactly 0.0%. This is a surprising result, suggesting the hijacking signal entirely bypasses direct attention to the chaos tokens. What is your hypothesized alternative pathway for the signal in the IT model?

**Missing References:**

**Justification:**
This paper presents a significant and concerning result for the field of AI alignment: that instruction tuning, a cornerstone of modern safety practices, can make models *representationally* more vulnerable to subtle manipulation while making them *behaviorally* appear more robust. The work is a model of excellent science, combining a novel hypothesis with a rigorous, multi-layered mechanistic analysis that includes causal interventions, scaling laws, and cross-family replication. While there are opportunities to further strengthen the evidence for some claims, the core finding is well-established and of immediate importance to the community.
